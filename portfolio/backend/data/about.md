## Summary

Vatsal Vala is an AI/ML developer who focuses on making models work outside the notebook — in production, where real users, messy documents, and token budgets all push back. He currently works at Vivansh Infotech in Ahmedabad, building production RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) systems end-to-end.

Vatsal cares about the numbers behind the demo: he cut AI form generation to about 15 seconds and dropped token usage 60% (from 25k to 10k tokens) by re-architecting a two-stage LLM flow, and he tunes full retrieval chains (chunking, MiniLM embeddings in Pinecone, top-3 reranking) so GPT-4o answers from context instead of guessing.

Before his professional role, Vatsal built projects for the joy of it: he was a Grand Finalist at the SSIP New India Vibrant Hackathon, built Dhanvantari (an AI assistant for Ayurvedic consultations), and created a sign-language translator that reads gestures in real time at 92% accuracy.

He completed his Bachelor of Engineering in AI & Data Science at Government Engineering College (GEC) Rajkot, and works end-to-end — from backend to retrieval to the prompt that makes it all click.
